What Are Meta Tags?

Meta tags are HTML elements that provide structured metadata about a web page. They sit in the <head> of your HTML and tell search engines, browsers, and social platforms what your page is about.

The most important meta tags fall into three categories:

  1. SEO tags — control how your page appears in search results
  2. Open Graph (OG) tags — control how your page appears on Facebook, LinkedIn, WhatsApp, Slack
  3. Twitter Card tags — control how your page appears on X/Twitter

Essential Meta Tags (You Need These)

1. Title Tag

<title>SEO Meta Tags Guide — Free Generator & Preview Tool</title>

The title tag is the most important SEO element. It appears as the clickable headline in search results. Best practices:

  • 50-60 characters — Google typically displays the first 50-60 characters
  • Primary keyword first — Put your most important keyword at the beginning
  • Unique per page — Every page should have a unique title
  • Include brand — Add your brand name at the end (separated by a pipe or dash)

2. Meta Description

<meta name="description" content="Generate and preview Open Graph, Twitter Card, and SEO meta tags. See how your page looks on Google, Facebook, and X/Twitter. Free online tool.">

The meta description is the snippet shown below your title in search results. It doesn't directly affect rankings, but it dramatically impacts click-through rates.

  • 150-160 characters — Google typically truncates after ~160 characters
  • Include a call-to-action — "Learn how," "Get started," "Try free"
  • Match search intent — Address what the searcher wants
  • Include target keyword — Bolded keywords catch attention

Twitter Cards make your tweets look professional. Four card types:

  • summary — Small thumbnail (left side of card)
  • summary_large_image — Large image at top (recommended)
  • app — Mobile app download card
  • player — Video/audio player card

Common Meta Tag Mistakes

  1. Missing image — Without og:image, platforms show a blank card
  2. Wrong image size — Too small images get stretched or cropped
  3. Mismatched title and OG title — They should be the same or very similar
  4. Description too long — Platforms truncate, so keep OG descriptions under 200 characters
  5. Not using canonical URL — Without it, www and non-www versions compete
  6. Special characters in titles — Some platforms mangle HTML entities in OG tags

Advanced Tips

For E-commerce Sites

Use og:type="product" with additional tags:

<meta property="product:price:amount" content="29.99">
<meta property="product:price:currency" content="USD">

For Blog Articles

<meta property="article:published_time" content="2025-06-15">
<meta property="article:author" content="Author Name">
<meta property="article:section" content="SEO">

For Videos

<meta property="og:video" content="https://yoursite.com/video.mp4">
<meta property="og:video:width" content="1920">
<meta property="og:video:height" content="1080">
