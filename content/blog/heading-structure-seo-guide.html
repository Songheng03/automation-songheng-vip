How to Structure Your H1 and H2 Tags for Better Search Rankings

Published June 12, 2025 · 6 min read · Free SEO Audit Tool

Heading tags (H1 through H6) are one of the most important on-page SEO elements — yet they're also one of the most commonly misused. This guide covers exactly how to structure them correctly.

Why Heading Structure Matters for SEO

Search engines use heading tags to understand the structure and hierarchy of your content. Think of headings as an outline:

  • H1 = The page title / main topic
  • H2 = Major section headings
  • H3 = Sub-sections under H2s
  • H4-H6 = Further nesting (rarely needed)

When your heading structure is logical, search engines can better understand what your page is about — and rank it for relevant queries.

The Cardinal Rule: One H1 Per Page

✅ Correct: One H1 that matches your title tag
❌ Wrong: Multiple H1s, or no H1 at all

Your H1 should be the single most descriptive title for the page. It should closely match (or be identical to) your <title> tag. This creates consistency between what search engines show in results and what users see on the page.

The Heading Hierarchy

Never skip levels. Going from H1 directly to H3 confuses both users and search engines:

✅ Correct hierarchy:
H1: How to Bake Sourdough Bread
H2: Ingredients You'll Need
H3: Choosing the Right Flour
H3: Water Temperature Matters
H2: The Fermentation Process
❌ Wrong (skipped level):
H1: How to Bake Sourdough Bread
H3: Choosing Flour ← skipped H2!

Keywords in Headings

Include your primary keyword in the H1. Include related keywords in H2s where natural. But always prioritize readability — keyword stuffing in headings looks spammy and hurts rankings.

Common Mistakes

  1. Multiple H1s — Only one per page. Ever.
  2. No H1 — Every page needs exactly one.
  3. Using headings just for visual styling — Use CSS for font size, not heading tags.
  4. Empty headings — Every heading should describe the content below it.
  5. H1 mismatch — H1 should match or closely relate to the title tag.
  6. Heading keyword stuffing — Write for humans first.
