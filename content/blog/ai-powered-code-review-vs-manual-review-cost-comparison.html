But What About Quality?

The most common pushback: "AI doesn't understand business context." It's true — AI can't tell you if a variable name matches your team's internal conventions. But for everything else, it's surprisingly good:

What AI Catches Better

  • Security vulnerabilities: SQL injection, XSS, CSRF, hardcoded secrets, unsafe deserialization — AI has seen millions of vulnerable code patterns in training data
  • Edge cases: Null pointer dereferences, off-by-one errors, race conditions
  • Best practices: OWASP Top 10, language-specific idioms, performance anti-patterns
  • Consistency: AI never gets tired, never rushes through a Friday afternoon PR

What Humans Still Do Better

  • Architecture decisions: "Should we use microservices or monorepo?"
  • Team convention enforcement: Unwritten rules about code organization
  • High-level design feedback: "This approach won't scale to 10M users"

The optimal approach? AI does first pass (5¢, 5 seconds). Human reviews the AI report, addressing only the real issues. This cuts human review time by 60–70% while catching more bugs.

Why the Economics Favor AI

Code review is fundamentally a pattern-matching problem. A human reviewer scans code, spots patterns they've seen before (bugs, vulnerabilities, style issues), and flags them. This is exactly what LLMs excel at.

Meanwhile, the cost of human attention keeps rising. Senior engineer salaries in 2026 are up 8% year-over-year. The opportunity cost of context switching — pulling a $165k engineer out of flow to review a PR — is higher than ever.
