Why You Need a Sitemap

A sitemap is a roadmap for search engine crawlers. When you publish a new page, a sitemap helps Google discover it faster. Without one, you're relying entirely on internal links and backlinks — which can take days or weeks for new content.

Sitemaps are especially important for:

  • New websites — Google needs to find your pages
  • Large sites — 100+ pages that crawlers might miss
  • Frequently updated content — blogs, news, product listings
  • Isolated pages — pages with few internal links

What the Tool Does

Our sitemap generator creates a valid XML sitemap following the sitemaps.org protocol. You provide:

  • Your website's base URL
  • A list of paths (e.g., /blog, /about)
  • Default change frequency (daily, weekly, monthly)
  • Default priority (0.0–1.0)

It generates a complete sitemap with proper <loc>, <lastmod>, <changefreq>, and <priority> tags — ready to upload to your server.

Example Output

<?xml version="1.0" encoding="UTF-8"?>
<urlset xmlns="http://www.sitemaps.org/schemas/sitemap/0.9">
  <url>
    <loc>https://example.com/</loc>
    <lastmod>2025-06-14</lastmod>
    <changefreq>daily</changefreq>
    <priority>1.0</priority>
  </url>
</urlset>

How to Use Your Sitemap

1. Generate It

Open the sitemap generator tool, paste your URLs, choose your settings, and click Generate. Download the file or copy it to clipboard.

2. Upload to Your Server

Place the sitemap.xml file in your site's root directory. If you're using a static host like Netlify, Vercel, or GitHub Pages, just add it to your repo's public folder.

3. Submit to Search Engines

Google: Open Search Console → Sitemaps → Enter your sitemap URL.
Bing: Use the Bing Webmaster Tools sitemap section.

4. Reference in robots.txt

Sitemap: https://yourwebsite.com/sitemap.xml

Adding this line to your robots.txt helps every search engine discover your sitemap automatically.
