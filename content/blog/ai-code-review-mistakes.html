AI Code Review: What Developers Get Wrong About Automated Review

Published June 10, 2025 · 5 min read · Try Free Code Review

AI code review tools can catch bugs, improve consistency, and speed up development. But many developers use them wrong. Here are the most common mistakes:

1. Treating AI Review as Final Authority

AI suggestions are just suggestions. Always apply your own judgment — especially for architecture decisions and business logic.

2. Not Providing Context

AI reviewers work better when they understand your stack, coding standards, and what to look for. The more context you give, the better the results.

3. Ignoring False Positives

Some AI suggestions will be wrong. Instead of blindly accepting or rejecting, learn what kinds of mistakes the AI makes in your specific codebase.

4. Using AI as a Replacement for Human Review

AI excels at catching style issues, potential bugs, and security vulnerabilities. But it can't understand business context, team dynamics, or long-term maintenance tradeoffs. Use both.

5. Not Using AI for the Right Things

AI is great for: security scanning, style consistency, documentation gaps, test coverage. It's less good for: architecture decisions, API design, performance optimization.
